01 / Font Pairing
Raleway + Inter

Raleway is a geometric display sans-serif — distinctive at large sizes, but its thin strokes and tight apertures reduce readability in body paragraphs. Inter is screen-optimized with a high x-height and open apertures, built for long reading at small sizes.

Same pattern used by Linear, Stripe, and Vercel: one brand typeface for headlines, one workhorse for everything else.

14 / Voice Examples
Good Copy vs Bad Copy

Quiet executive relief. Short, confident, outcome-first. Never stack buzzwords or use exclamation marks.

Hero Headline
Don't
"The Ultimate AI-Powered Analytics Revolution"
3 banned words. Vague. No outcome.
Do
"Connect once. The Agent handles the rest."
Specific. Outcome-first. 8 words.
Feature Card
Don't
"Our revolutionary platform leverages cutting-edge AI to deliver best-in-class insights for your team."
Buzzword stack. First-person "our".
Do
"500+ data sources. One knowledge graph. Insights arrive before you ask."
Specific numbers. Benefit. "You" language.
Pain Section
Don't
"Are you tired of broken dashboards? We have the solution!"
Questions invite hesitation. Exclamation mark. First-person.
Do
"72% of analyst time goes to data prep. Not analysis."
Stat-led. Short. Lets the number do the work.
Stat Caption
Don't
"We helped Activision Blizzard save money on their analytics operations"
First-person "we". Vague "money".
Do
"$2.4M saved — Activision Blizzard"
Quantified. Third-person. 5 words.
